Types of Markings

Taking into account the variety of surfaces on which the markings are to be made, as well as the expectations of our customers regarding the durability of the horizontal markings we make, we use different types of horizontal markings.

We use spray painting to create horizontal markings. The choice of the method of applying the lines depends on many factors. If the facilities have unfavorable temperatures or the stripes are exposed to mechanical or chemical abrasion, we use indentation marking, which is the most durable method of marking the substrate. In other cases, we use less invasive and much more economical marking methods for the floor. Regardless of the method chosen, we carry out the work with extraordinary accuracy and compliance with quality standards and legally required standards.

Below you will find technical details regarding the different types of horizontal markings available in our offer:

Surface Markings

Thickness: 0.2 - 0.3 MM

Created by machine applying two layers of paint (primer + main layer) at a pressure of 15 bar onto a previously cleaned and degreased concrete or resin floor.

Paint Options:
- 2-component chemically cured resin paints (epoxy/polyurethane): durable and abrasion-resistant. Drying time: 10 to 24 hours.
- Solvent-based (or water-based) acrylic resin paints: drying time: 20 min to 2 hrs.

Surface Markings on a Shot-Blasted Substrate

Thickness: 0.4 - 0.6 MM

Created by mechanically removing the top layer of the concrete floor (approx. 200 microns) using a shot blaster equipped with a special 10 cm wide head. This removes all impurities and develops the contact surface of the floor and paint.

Paint Options:
- 2-component chemically cured paints (epoxy/polyurethane): durable and abrasion-resistant. Drying time: 12 to 24 hours.

In-Depth Markings

Depth: 2 - 3 MM

Created by precise machine cutting using diamond discs in the concrete substrate of a groove 5 to 20 cm wide and up to 3 mm deep. The groove is filled with colored 2-component epoxy resin.

Depth marking is virtually indestructible.
